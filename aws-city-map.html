District 01 · Global

City Hall — Identity & Access

Before any road, building, or gate exists, City Hall decides who's allowed to do what. IAM isn't a service you visit — it's the permission slip every other building checks first.

IAM User

🪪Resident ID

One identity per person. Never a shared badge — if John needs access, John gets his own IAM User.

IAM Group

🏘️Neighborhood Association

Bundles residents (Developers, Security Team) so permissions are granted once, inherited by everyone in it.

IAM Policy

📜Zoning Rules

A JSON document answering "what are you allowed to do here?" Allow, deny — nothing more mysterious than that.

Least Privilege

🔑Master-Key Policy

Hand out the fewest keys possible. An intern uploading files doesn't need a key to the database vault.

IAM Role

🎫Visitor Day-Pass

A temporary identity, not a permanent key. EC2 borrows a role to reach S3; the pass expires — nothing to leak.

District 03 · Regional Services

The Harbor — Storage Outside the Walls

S3 and DynamoDB aren't buildings inside your VPC at all — they're the harbor warehouses everyone in the region shares. A private building reaching them shouldn't sail out onto the open internet, so it uses a private bridge instead.

S3

🏬The Warehouse District

Object storage, not a hard drive — files plus metadata, spread across a region. 11 nines durability: nearly impossible to lose a crate.

S3 Storage Classes

🗄️Warehouse Aisles

Standard (front shelf) → Infrequent Access → Glacier (basement archive) → Deep Archive (off-site vault, cheapest, slowest).

S3 Versioning

🕰️Receipt Archive

Keeps every past copy of a file — delete something by mistake, pull the old version back off the shelf.

DynamoDB

📇The Instant Lookup Desk

NoSQL key-value store, scales itself, built for millions of quick lookups rather than complex joins.

Redshift

📊The Analytics Library

A data warehouse for huge reports and BI — not where your live application reads and writes.

Gateway VPC Endpoint

🌉Private Harbor Bridge

Free, direct road from a private subnet straight to S3 / DynamoDB — never touches the open internet.

Interface Endpoint (PrivateLink)

🚇Private Tunnel

Same idea for most other AWS services, using a private IP inside your VPC. Paid, but never public.

District 04 · Local Storage

What's in the Basement

Not every drive lives in the harbor. Some storage is bolted straight to a building.

EBS

💽The Building's Own Hard Drive

Block storage that can't exist without its EC2 — like a laptop's internal SSD. Low latency, one building at a time.

EFS

🗂️Shared Filing Room

A folder many EC2 instances open at once — Linux only, grows automatically. Think a shared drive, not a private disk.

Instance Store

📦The Loading Dock

Physical disk bolted to the server itself — blazing fast, but the moment the instance stops, everything on the dock disappears.

District 05 · The Postal Service

Messaging — Buildings That Don't Talk Directly

Good city planning means a factory shouldn't shout directly at a shop across town — it drops a message in a system built to hold, route, or broadcast it.

SQS

📬The Mail Queue

Messages wait in line, handled one at a time. If a worker crashes mid-delivery, the letter simply stays in the box.

SNS

📣The Town Crier

One announcement, many subscribers hear it at once — one-to-many broadcast.

EventBridge

🚦The Traffic Dispatcher

"If this happens, route it there." Event-driven automation between services.

Step Functions

🧭The Foreman's Checklist

Chains Lambda functions into an ordered workflow, with retries and decisions built in.

API Gateway

🚪The Front Desk

The one door visitors knock on before reaching Lambda — checks ID, throttles crowds, logs who came in.

District 06 · Hybrid & Multi-VPC

The Transit Hub & the Road Out of Town

Big companies rarely have one city. This is how separate VPCs, and the office back on-premises, connect to this one.

VPC Peering

🌁Private Footbridge

A direct one-to-one bridge between two VPCs. Not transitive — if A connects to B and B to C, A still can't reach C.

Transit Gateway

✈️The Airport Hub

Instead of a bridge to every VPC individually, everyone connects to one hub — like flying through Atlanta instead of point-to-point.

Site-to-Site VPN

🚗The Encrypted Backroad

Cheap, easy tunnel over the public internet connecting your office to AWS. Slower, but gets the job done.

Direct Connect

🚄The Private Railway

A dedicated physical cable from your office to AWS — lower latency, higher bandwidth, and a real install project.

Hybrid Cloud

🏢The Satellite Office

Some buildings never left the old office park. VPN or Direct Connect is the commute between the two campuses.

District 07 · Global Edge

The Outer Ring — Reaching the Whole World

Outside the metro area entirely: a ring of small delivery outposts and the phone book everyone uses to find the city in the first place.

Route 53

📖The City Phone Book

Translates names ("example.com") into addresses. Routing policies decide who gets sent where — simple, weighted, latency, failover, geolocation.

CloudFront

🚚Local Delivery Depots

Copies your content to Edge Locations near each visitor, so a user in Australia isn't downloading from Virginia every time.

Global Accelerator

🛰️The Express Lane

Speeds up live, non-cacheable traffic — gaming, voice, trading — rather than caching static files like CloudFront.

District 08 · Oversight

The Watchtowers

A city needs eyes on it — not to stop traffic, but to know exactly what happened, and when.

CloudWatch

🗼The Weather Vane

Metrics, logs, dashboards, alarms — CPU, memory, request counts, error rates, watched continuously.

CloudTrail

🎥The City's Security Camera

Records every API call: who changed what, when, and from where. The first place investigators look.

AWS Config

📐The Building Inspector

Tracks configuration changes over time and flags anything drifting from the approved blueprint.

Scaling Vertical = a bigger building. Horizontal = more buildings, load-balanced — AWS's preferred way to grow.
High Availability Never one building, one AZ, or one road. Multi-AZ, Auto Scaling, and replicas are how the city survives a blackout.
Disaster Recovery Backup & Restore (cheap, slow) → Pilot Light → Warm Standby → Multi-Site (fast, expensive) — a second city, always ready.
Well-Architected Pillars Operational Excellence · Security · Reliability · Performance Efficiency · Cost Optimization · Sustainability — the six laws of good city planning.
The One Question For every building on this map, ask: what problem does it solve, and who's allowed to knock on its door?
